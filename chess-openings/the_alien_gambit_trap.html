Things get truly interesting after Black captures on e4. The White knight coolly recaptures, setting the stage for a pivotal moment in this cosmic clash. In this universally familiar position, Black typically develops the knight to f6. Black could opt for 2…Bf5, but let’s not veer off course. Although Nf6 invites Ng5, the Alien Gambit’s true trap springs only after Black parries with…h6. Technically, h6 isn’t a blunder—it’s a logical move to kick the aggressive knight. This is where White throws the gauntlet, offering a knight to lure Black’s king out of hiding. Black has to bite; otherwise, they lose even more material. King takes, and we’re off to the races. The subtlety here is key—White preserves attacking flexibility, perhaps eyeing Ne5+ to draw the Black king further out before considering Bc4.
